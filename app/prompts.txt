THE PSYCHOLOGY
This works because of The Endowment Effect:

People value things they already "own"
After 4 workouts, they feel like it's "their program"
They've invested time and effort
Losing access feels like a loss

Combined with Sunk Cost Fallacy:

"I already did 4 workouts, I'm not starting over"
Much more likely to pay than abandon


POTENTIAL ISSUES & SOLUTIONS
Issue 1: People gaming the system
Problem: User does 4 free workouts, creates new account, repeats
Solution:

Require email verification
Track by device ID (not just account)
After 4 workouts across all accounts on that device, paywall appears

Issue 2: Too generous?
Problem: 4 workouts might be too much free value
Counter: No! 4 workouts = ~2 weeks. Perfect sweet spot.

1-2 workouts: Not enough to see results or build habit
4 workouts: Just enough to get hooked
8+ workouts: Too generous, they might not need more

Issue 3: They abandon after workout 4
Problem: 70% of users quit at the paywall
Solution:

Email sequence: "We noticed you stopped at workout 4..."
Retargeting: "Come back and see your progress"
Limited-time offer: "20% off if you return this week"


PRICING STRATEGY FOR THIS MODEL
Since they're already invested, you can charge more than typical:
Per Program:

$39-$79 depending on length (8-16 weeks)
"Unlock this program: $49"

OR - All Access Pass (better):

$9.99/month or $79/year
"Unlimited access to all programs"
They might buy one program for $49, or get all for $79/year

My recommendation: Offer both

Buy individual program: $49
OR get All Access: $9.99/month
Most will choose All Access (better lifetime value for you)
